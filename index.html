A.P.J. Abdul Kalam
Great Indian scientist and politician who played a leading rile in the development of india's missile and nuclear weapons programs.

About the Legend

☛ A.P.J. Abdul kalam, in full Avul pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, was born on october 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, Indian.

☛ He Served ad the 11th president of India from 2002 to 2007.

☛ Kalam earned a degree in aeronautical engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology and in 1958 joined the Defence Reasearch and Development Organisation (DRDO).

☛ In 1996, he moved to the Indian Space Reasearch Organization, where he was project director of the SLV-III, the first satellite launch vehicle that was both designed and produced in India.

☛ Rejoining DRDO in 1982, Kalam planned the program that produced a number of successful missiles, which helped earn him the nickname "Missile Man."

☛ Among those success was Agni, India's first intermediate-range ballistic missile, whjhich incorporated aspects of the SLV-III and was launched in 1989.

☛ He aslo played a pivotal organisational, technical, and political role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear test in 1998, the first since the original nuclear test by india in 1974.

☛ From 1992 to 1997 Kalam was scientific adviser the defense minister, and he later served as principal scientific adviser (1999-2001) to the government with the rank of cabinet minister.

☛ His prominent role in the country's 1998 nuclear weapons tests solidified India as a national hero, althoigh the tests caused great concern in the international community.

☛ In 1998 Kalam put forward a countrywide plan called Technology Vision 2020, which he described as a road map for transforming India from a less-developed to a developed society in years. The plan called for, among other measures, increasing agricultural productivity, emphasizing technology as a vehicle for economic growth, and widening access to health care and education.

☛ Kalam received 7 honorary doctorates from 40 universities. The Government of India honoures him with the Oadma Bhusan in 1981 and the Padma Vibhushan in 1990 for his work with ISRO and DRDO and his role as a scientific advisor to the Government.

☛ In 1997, Kalam received India's highest civilian honour, the Bharat Ratna, for his contribution to the scientific reasearch and modernisation of defence technology inn India.

☛ In 2013, he was the recipient of the von barun Award from the National Space Society "to recognize excellence inn the management and leadership of space-realted project".

☛ While delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong, Kalam collapsed and died from an apparent cardiac arrest on 27 july 2015, aged 83.

☛ Wheeler Island, a natinal missile test site in Oddisha, was renamed Kalam Island in September 2015.

☛ A prominent road in New Delhi was renamed from Aurangzeb Road toDr APJ Abdul Kalam Road in August 2015.

☛ In February 2018, scintists from the botanical Survey of India named newly found plant species as Drypetes kalamii, in his honour.
